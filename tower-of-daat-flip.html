Chapter 1
The First Step

At the edge of the desert, where the night sky lowered like a curtain of ink, the Tower of Da’at rose from the sand. It was not built of stone, but of letters—Aleph to Tav—stacked and woven into a single, shimmering pillar.

Each letter burned softly, pulsing like a heartbeat. Anyone who drew near could feel them thrumming under the skin, as if the alphabet remembered the person before the person remembered themselves.

2

He placed his hand upon the lowest gate—Aleph joined with Bet— and the letters brightened in welcome. Somewhere within the spine of the tower, a bell made only of light rang once.

“Every step is a question,” the tower seemed to whisper. “Every answer is another step.” And so he began to climb.
